Pico do Arieiro to Pico Ruivo

The Pico do Arieiro to Pico Ruivo trek is one of Madeira’s most iconic high-altitude trails, famous for its dramatic ridgelines, knife-edge paths, and ever-shifting cloud inversions. The route links two of the island’s highest peaks and involves steep ascents, tunnels carved into volcanic rock, and panoramic views over Madeira’s rugged interior. According to many hikers—my parents included, who have walked portions of it—it is breathtakingly beautiful. Despite Madeira’s small size, this trail is often considered one of the most striking mountain walks in Europe, combining subtropical climate with alpine-style terrain. Historically, the high paths between these peaks were used by local shepherds and farmers navigating the island’s central massif. Today, the route continues this legacy as part of Madeira’s well-maintained network of veredas and levada trails, supporting sustainable tourism while preserving the island’s cultural and natural heritage.

10km. 5 hrs.
