Nomad Valley Trail

Kyrgyzstan is an incredibly special place to me, and is high on my list of countries to visit; it is one of the first countries my parents lived in together. My dad works for the United Nations, and so my parents often ended up, over the course of their marriage, in far-flung corners of the world (alluding, slightly, to the name of this collection of trails). Their photographs (although not shared here, as they are rather private/personal family material) recount rolling green hills, velvety soft, impossibly sharp mountain peaks — black combs against cerulean skies — and incredibly kind people.

The Nomad Valley Trail is a section of the longer Kyrgyz Nomad Trail in Kyrgyzstan, known for its challenging multi-day treks through remote, alpine scenery with yurt camps. According to my parents, who have hiked sections of this trail, it is incredibly beautiful. Shockingly — due to its nation’s barely-touristic nature — the Kyrgyz Nomad Trail is the longest network of trails in Central Asia. These trails were originally used by nomadic people for migration and trade, with their history stretching back centuries for routes like the Salt Route. Modern trails are often a continuation of this legacy, now developed for sustainable tourism while preserving cultural heritage.

56km. 3.5 days avg.
