The Fishermen's Trail

The Fishermen's Trail (Rota Vicentina) in Portugal is a coastal hiking route that traces the paths once used by local fishermen to access remote fishing spots along the Alentejo and Algarve coasts. This trail has a rich history dating back to the early 20th century when fishing was a primary livelihood for many coastal communities in the region. The paths were originally informal tracks, created by generations of fishermen who needed to navigate the rugged coastline to reach their boats and fishing grounds. Over time, these trails became well-trodden routes, connecting small villages and providing access to secluded beaches and coves.
